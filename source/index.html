I'm a writer, theorist, social scientist, teacher, militant. In Fall 2013 I joined the faculty of Politics and International Relations at the University of Southampton, UK. Nearly all of my intellectual and political work revolves around the puzzle: why is there not more resistance to the status quo? How do institutions and ideological apparatuses pacify various types of political resistance? As an academic researcher so far I have mostly focused on political economy and mass media, with a longer-term view of bringing quantitative methods to bear on unsolved riddles from the revolutionary tradition. Before I came to the UK, I did my Ph.D. at Temple University in the US, where I was active in Occupy Wall Street, some civil disobedience and shutting down of things, some longer-term campaigns against the big U.S. banks, and sundry other works and deeds, including a radical warehouse project where I lived for nearly three years.
