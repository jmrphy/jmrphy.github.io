I’m a writer, theorist, applied statistician, radical militant, and teacher. In Fall 2013 I join the faculty of Politics and International Relations at the University of Southampton, UK. Nearly all of my work as an intellectual and militant revolves around the puzzle of why there is not more resistance to capitalism. As an academic researcher I focus on poltical economy and mass media, with a longer-term view of bringing quantitative methods to bear on unsolved riddles from the radical and revolutionary traditions. Before I came to the UK, I did my Ph.D. at Temple University in the United States, where I was active in the Occupy Wall Street movement, some civil disobedience, a longer-term campaign against out-of-control U.S. banks, and an experimental warehouse project where I lived for nearly three years.
